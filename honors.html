Honors Programs

Enhance your transfer journey with Honors opportunities at California Community Colleges. Priority admission, early registration, and exclusive benefits.

What is the Honors Program?

The Honors Program at California Community Colleges provides academically motivated students with an enriched educational experience designed to prepare them for transfer to competitive four-year institutions.

Honors Transfer Council of California (HTCC) & TAP

The Honors Transfer Council of California (HTCC) coordinates honors programs across California community colleges. Many colleges participate in the Transfer Alliance Program (TAP), which provides additional benefits for UC transfers, particularly to UCLA.

Why Join an Honors Program?

Priority Admission

Enhanced consideration during the UC and CSU transfer application review process

Early Registration

Register for classes before other students, ensuring you get the courses you need

Smaller Classes

Honors courses typically have smaller class sizes for more personalized attention

Research Mentoring

Access to research opportunities and faculty mentorship programs

Enriched Curriculum

Challenging coursework that prepares you for university-level academics

Networking

Connect with other high-achieving students and build lasting relationships

Application Timeline & Getting Started

Honors program application timelines vary by college. Check with your specific community college for:

  • Application deadlines (typically in spring for fall entry)
  • Minimum GPA requirements (usually 3.0-3.5)
  • Required essays or personal statements
  • Faculty recommendations
  • Interview requirements (if applicable)

Pro Tip: Many students apply during their first semester at community college. Don't wait—start early!
