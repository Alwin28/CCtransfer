What is the Honors Program?

The Honors Program at California Community Colleges provides academically motivated students with an enriched educational experience designed to prepare them for transfer to competitive four-year institutions.

The Honors Transfer Council of California (HTCC) coordinates honors programs across California community colleges. Many colleges participate in the Transfer Alliance Program (TAP), which provides additional benefits for UC transfers, particularly to UCLA.

Benefits of Honors Programs

Priority Admission

Enhanced consideration during UC and CSU transfer applications

Early Registration

Register for classes before other students

Smaller Classes

More personalized attention in honors courses

Research Opportunities

Access to faculty mentorship and research programs

Enriched Curriculum

Challenging coursework for university preparation

Student Community

Connect with motivated, high-achieving peers

Application Timeline

Honors program application timelines vary by college. Check with your specific community college for:

  • Application deadlines (typically spring for fall entry)
  • Minimum GPA requirements (usually 3.0-3.5)
  • Required essays or personal statements
  • Faculty recommendations
  • Interview requirements (if applicable)

Tip: Many students apply during their first semester. Start early.
